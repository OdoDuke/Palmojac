Palmojac Workout

Your Health Your Wealth

Age 1 to 10 (Children): For children in this age group, focus on activities that promote physical development, motor skills, and overall fun.

   Jumping jacks: 2 sets of 10 repetitions

Running or jogging: 5-10 minutes

Playground activities: Climbing, swinging, sliding, etc. - 20-30 minutes

Dancing: 10-15 minutes

Stretching exercises: Gentle stretches for 5-10 minutes

Age 11 to 20 (Teenagers):
For teenagers, focus on a mix of cardio, strength, and flexibility exercises.

   Warm-up: Jumping jacks or skipping rope - 5 minutes

Cardio: Running, cycling, or sports activities - 20-30 minutes

Strength training: Bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, lunges - 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions each

Flexibility: Yoga or static stretches - 15-20 minutes

Cool down: Gentle stretches and deep breathing - 5 minutes

Age 21 to 30 (Young Adults):
For young adults, the focus should be on maintaining overall fitness and building strength.



   Warm-up: Jump rope or brisk walk - 5-10 minutes

Cardio: Running, cycling, swimming, or group sports - 30-45 minutes

Strength training: Compound exercises like bench press, deadlifts, squats - 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions each

Core exercises: Planks, crunches, leg raises - 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions each

Cool down: Yoga or stretching - 10-15 minutes

Age 31 to 40 (Adults):
For adults, the focus is on maintaining fitness, managing stress, and preventing injuries.

   Warm-up: Jumping jacks, jogging, or brisk walking - 5-10 minutes

Cardio: Running, cycling, swimming, or aerobics - 30-45 minutes

Strength training: Full-body exercises with weights or resistance bands - 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions each

Flexibility: Dynamic stretches or yoga - 15-20 minutes

Cool down: Gentle stretches and relaxation - 10 minutes

Age 41 to 50 (Middle-aged Adults):
For middle-aged adults, the focus should be on maintaining strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular health.

  Warm-up: Brisk walk or light jogging - 5-10 minutes

Cardio: Any low-impact activity like swimming, cycling, or dancing - 30-45 minutes

Strength training: Compound exercises with lighter weights - 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions each

Flexibility: Yoga or static stretches - 15-20 minutes

Cool down: Gentle stretches and deep breathing - 10 minutes
